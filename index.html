About

I am an associate professor at Department of Statistics, Harvard University, in Edo Airoldi's applied statistics lab.

I enjoy solving problems that require statistics, math and programming. If networks are involved or the data is big, that is even better. I have been working on genomic and proteomic data sets and social networks.

I participate in the XDATA Darpa project, and have implemented several network analysis methods in the open source software library igraph.
